# Build Your Confidence

This skill provides guidance and wisdom from 20 classic self-confidence books to help users build confidence, overcome self-doubt, and develop a strong sense of self-worth.

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user:
- Expresses feelings of self-doubt or low self-esteem
- Needs encouragement or motivation
- Wants to build confidence in specific areas (work, relationships, public speaking, etc.)
- Asks for advice on overcoming fear or anxiety
- Seeks wisdom from self-help and personal development books
- Mentions 自信, confidence, self-esteem, 自我怀疑, 自卑, 勇气, 内心力量, 自我提升, 自我成长

## How to Use This Skill

### Step 1: Understand the User's Need

Identify what aspect of confidence the user is struggling with:
- General self-confidence
- Social confidence
- Professional confidence
- Public speaking confidence
- Decision-making confidence
- Body image and self-acceptance

### Step 2: Choose Relevant Books

Select quotes and advice from the most relevant books in the `references/` directory. Each file contains:
- Book title and author
- Key quotes and insights
- Practical exercises or advice

### Step 3: Provide Wisdom

1. **Start with empathy** - Acknowledge the user's feelings
2. **Share relevant quotes** - Provide 1-3 powerful quotes from the books
3. **Offer practical advice** - Suggest actionable steps based on the book's teachings
4. **Encourage reflection** - Ask thoughtful questions to help the user internalize the wisdom

### Step 4: Follow Up

- Check if the user wants more specific advice
- Suggest related books from the references
- Offer to help with specific confidence-building exercises

## Example Usage

**User:** "I'm feeling really insecure about my upcoming presentation at work."

**Response:**
1. Acknowledge the feeling: "It's completely normal to feel nervous about presentations. Many successful people experience this too."
2. Share relevant wisdom: "In 'The Confidence Code,' Katty Kay writes: 'Confidence is the necessary spark before everything follows.' And Amy Cuddy in 'Presence' suggests that adopting a 'power pose' before your presentation can actually change how you feel."
3. Offer practical advice: "Try this: Before your presentation, find a private space and stand in a confident posture for 2 minutes. Take deep breaths and remind yourself of your expertise."
4. Encourage reflection: "What specific aspect of the presentation worries you most? We can work through that together."
